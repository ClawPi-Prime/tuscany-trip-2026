# 🍽️ Restaurant Tips – Lucca & Surroundings

Diet: Pescatarian adults. Kids need simple pasta/pizza friendly places.
All listed restaurants are family-friendly or noted otherwise.

---

## In Lucca (centro storico)

### Buca di Sant'Antonio
- **Type:** Classic Lucchese trattoria, one of the oldest in the city (founded 1782)
- **Why go:** Iconic, great fish dishes, proper local cuisine
- **Good for:** Dinner without kids — a bit formal
- **Address:** Via della Cervia 3, Lucca
- **Note:** Book ahead

### Osteria Baralla
- **Type:** Casual osteria
- **Why go:** Excellent local dishes, relaxed vibe, reasonable prices
- **Good for:** Lunch or dinner with kids
- **Address:** Via Anfiteatro 5, Lucca (right on Piazza dell'Anfiteatro)

### Trattoria da Leo
- **Type:** No-frills local trattoria
- **Why go:** Cheap, authentic, always packed with locals — great sign
- **Good for:** Casual lunch
- **Address:** Via Tegrimi 1, Lucca
- **Note:** Cash only, no reservations, arrive early

### Pizzeria da Felice
- **Type:** Street food / pizza al taglio
- **Why go:** Famous for *cecina* (chickpea flatbread) and *focaccia* — perfect quick lunch for kids
- **Address:** Via Buia 12, Lucca
- **Note:** Standing only, but the kids will love it

## Near Capannori / Hills

### Ristorante Butterfly (Porcari)
- **Type:** Upscale but family-friendly

- ~10 min from the house

### Locanda l'Elisa (Massa Pisana)
- **Type:** Romantic countryside restaurant

- **Good for:** Adults-only dinner if you can arrange babysitting

## Day Trip: Cinque Terre

If you go to Cinque Terre, **Monterosso** and **Vernazza** have the best seafood restaurants. Look for anywhere with fresh *acciughe* (anchovies) — it's the local specialty. Avoid tourist traps right at the train station.

---

## Practical Tips

- **Lunch vs dinner:** Italians eat lunch 12:30–2:30pm, dinner from 7:30pm. Many places are closed in between.
- **Coperto:** Expect a small cover charge per person (€1–3) — it's normal, not a scam.
- **Kids:** Ask for *pasta al pomodoro* (pasta with tomato sauce) or *pasta al burro* (butter) — universally available and universally liked by 5-year-olds.
- **Gelato rule:** Gelateria Artigianale = real gelato. Avoid places with mountains of fluorescent gelato in open tubs.
- **Best gelato in Lucca:** Gelateria Veneta (Via Vittorio Veneto) — locals' favourite.
