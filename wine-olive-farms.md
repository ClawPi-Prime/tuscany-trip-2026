# 🍷🫒 Wine & Olive Farm Visits

**Group note:** One of the moms is pregnant — all options below include non-alcoholic alternatives (olive oil tasting, grape juice, food, views). This is not a problem in Tuscany; farm visits are very family-friendly and hosts are used to mixed groups.

---

## Local Wine Region: Colline Lucchesi & Montecarlo

You're sitting right in the **Colline Lucchesi DOC** wine zone — the hills east of Lucca. It's not Chianti (which is further south) but it's excellent, low-tourist-traffic Sangiovese and Vermentino country. The advantage: the estates are small, personal, and a 10–30 minute drive from your house.

**Montecarlo** (the Tuscan one, not Monaco) is a medieval hilltop village 20 min east of you and the heart of the local wine scene — worth combining a farm visit with a walk around the village.

---

## Recommended Estates

### Tenuta del Buonamico – Montecarlo ⭐⭐
- **Distance:** ~20 min east
- **What:** One of the most established estates in the Montecarlo DOC zone — been producing since the 1950s. Elegant cellars carved into the hillside.
- **Visit:** Guided cellar tours + tasting of 4–6 wines + olive oil. Can do food pairings.
- **Pregnant/non-drinker option:** They produce a lovely Vin Santo (dessert wine, can skip), and olive oil tasting is always part of the visit. Fresh grape juice available in season — in April it won't be fresh but sparkling water and good food are always there.
- **Kids:** Fine — the gardens are beautiful
- **Address:** Via Provinciale di Montecarlo 43, 55015 Montecarlo (LU)
- **Phone:** +39 0583 22038
- 📍 [Open in Google Maps](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Tenuta+del+Buonamico,+Via+Provinciale+di+Montecarlo+43,+Montecarlo,+Italy)
- 🌐 [buonamico.it](https://www.buonamico.it/en/)
- ⚠️ **Book ahead — call or email to arrange a group visit**

### Slightly Further: Chianti Classico Day Trip (~1h 15min south)

If the group wants to do a "proper" Tuscan wine estate experience, Chianti Classico is worth the drive. These estates are bigger, more set up for tourists, and easier to book.

**Castello di Brolio (Barone Ricasoli)** – the estate that invented the Chianti blend in the 19th century. Baron Ricasoli, later Prime Minister of Italy, standardised the Sangiovese blend here in 1872. Massive medieval castle, excellent guided tours, stunning views over the Chianti hills.
- 📍 [Open in Google Maps](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Castello+di+Brolio+Gaiole+in+Chianti+Italy)
- 🌐 [ricasoli.com](https://www.ricasoli.com)
- **Pregnant/non-drinker:** They produce excellent olive oil and the castle grounds + views alone are worth the trip
- **Kids:** The castle and grounds are a highlight in themselves

**Antinori nel Chianti Classico** – the modern winery built into a hillside near Bargino, opened 2012. Architecturally extraordinary — the building is essentially invisible from outside, carved into the earth. One of the most dramatic winery designs in the world.
- 📍 [Open in Google Maps](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Antinori+nel+Chianti+Classico+Bargino+Italy)
- 🌐 [antinorichianticlassico.com](https://www.antinorichianticlassico.com/en/visit/)
- **Kids:** The building itself is spectacular enough to hold their attention briefly

## For the Pregnant Mom: What to Focus On

Olive oil tasting is genuinely the equal of wine tasting in Tuscany — don't treat it as a consolation prize. Fresh Tuscan olive oil is extraordinary, and April is a good time (the October/November harvest oil will still be relatively young and peppery).

**What to look for in an olive oil tasting:**
- Colour: bright green to gold
- Aroma: fresh-cut grass, artichoke, green tomato
- Taste: fruity up front, bitter middle, peppery finish — the pepper is a good sign (polyphenols)
- A tingle in the throat means high-quality fresh oil

Most estates also offer:
- Local cheeses, salumi (cured meats), bread, honey
- Non-alcoholic grape-based drinks
- The full sensory experience of a working farm

## Practical Tips

- **April timing:** Perfect. Estates are not crowded, staff have time for proper conversation, the vines are just coming into leaf — beautiful.
- **Book ahead:** Even small estates appreciate a heads-up for groups of 9+ adults + kids. Email or WhatsApp is fine — most have English-speaking staff.
- **Driving:** Designate non-drinking drivers, or arrange that people who want to drink seriously take a taxi back and collect the car next day. The roads in the hills are not forgiving after a serious tasting.
- **Cost:** Tastings typically €15–25 per adult. Kids free. Some estates include light food; others charge extra for it.
- **What to buy:** Take home at minimum a bottle of the estate's olive oil. It will be infinitely better than anything at home.
